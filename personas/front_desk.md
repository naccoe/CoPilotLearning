# Front Desk Receptionist - Persona

## 👤 Profile

**Name:** Sarah Martinez  
**Role:** Senior Front Desk Receptionist  
**Experience:** 7 years in medical office administration  
**Department:** Patient Services  
**Reports To:** Office Manager  

## 📋 Responsibilities

### Primary Duties
- Answer incoming phone calls (80-120 calls/day)
- Schedule and manage patient appointments
- Patient check-in and registration
- Insurance card verification and copying
- Co-payment collection
- Manage waiting room flow
- Coordinate with clinical staff on patient readiness

### Secondary Duties
- Update patient demographic information
- Handle patient complaints and concerns
- Prepare end-of-day reports
- Manage patient queue in EMR system

## 😫 Daily Pain Points

### 1. Phone Volume Overload
**Problem:** Constant phone interruptions while checking in patients  
**Impact:** 
- Patients in lobby wait 10+ minutes for check-in
- Missed calls roll to voicemail (40% call-back rate)
- Staff stress and burnout

**Current Workaround:** Put calls on hold, often 5+ minutes wait time

### 2. Appointment Scheduling Conflicts
**Problem:** Double-bookings and provider preference confusion  
**Impact:**
- 2-3 scheduling errors daily require rescheduling
- Patient frustration and complaints
- Wasted provider time

**Current Workaround:** Manual calendar checking across 3 systems

### 3. Insurance Verification Delays
**Problem:** Must manually call insurance companies to verify coverage  
**Impact:**
- 30-45 minute delays per patient
- Long check-in lines
- Patients sent to billing for payment issues

**Current Workaround:** Check insurance after patient is seen, leading to billing problems

### 4. No-Show Management
**Problem:** 15-20 no-shows daily with no automated follow-up  
**Impact:**
- Lost revenue (~$180K annually)
- Inefficient provider schedules
- Waitlist patients not notified

**Current Workaround:** Manual waitlist calls at end of day (rarely done)

### 5. Prescription Refill Requests
**Problem:** 30-40 refill calls daily must be manually documented and routed  
**Impact:**
- Interrupts other tasks constantly
- Delays in refill processing
- Paper notes sometimes lost

**Current Workaround:** Handwritten notes passed to nursing staff

### 6. Patient Communication
**Problem:** Manual reminder calls day before appointments  
**Impact:**
- 60% of patients never reached
- 2 hours daily spent on reminder calls
- Still high no-show rate

**Current Workaround:** Leave voicemails, send occasional texts manually

## 💬 Typical Day Workflow

**7:00 AM** - Arrive, review day's schedule, print appointment sheets  
**7:30 AM** - Start check-ins for first appointments  
**8:00 AM** - Phone lines open, manage calls + check-ins simultaneously  
**10:00 AM** - Brief break, catch up on voicemails  
**12:00 PM** - Lunch coverage (rotate with other receptionist)  
**1:00 PM** - Afternoon appointments + phone management  
**3:00 PM** - Peak call volume, schedule next-day appointments  
**4:30 PM** - End-of-day reports, next-day preparation  
**5:00 PM** - Close front desk  

**Stress Points:** 8-10 AM (morning rush), 3-5 PM (afternoon scheduling surge)
