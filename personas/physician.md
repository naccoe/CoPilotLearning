# Primary Care Physician - Persona

## 👤 Profile

**Name:** Dr. Emily Chen, MD  
**Role:** Primary Care Physician (Family Medicine)  
**Experience:** 12 years  
**Department:** Clinical Services  
**Board Certification:** American Board of Family Medicine  
**Patient Panel:** ~1,800 active patients  

## 📋 Responsibilities

### Primary Duties
- Patient consultations and examinations (18-22 patients/day)
- Diagnosis and treatment planning
- Prescription management and medication reviews
- Chronic disease management
- Preventive care and health maintenance
- Clinical documentation and coding
- Review lab results and imaging studies
- Coordinate specialist referrals

### Secondary Duties
- Supervise nurse practitioners and physician assistants
- Quality improvement initiatives
- Staff education and mentoring
- Patient portal message responses (20-30 daily)
- Prior authorization requests for medications and procedures

## 😫 Daily Pain Points

### 1. Documentation Burden
**Problem:** 2-3 hours of charting after clinic hours  
**Impact:**
- Work-life balance severely affected
- Burnout risk
- Reduced patient face time during visits
- Typing while patient talking (poor engagement)

**Current Workaround:** Stay until 7-8 PM to finish charts, work from home on weekends

### 2. Prior Authorization Hell
**Problem:** 10-15 prior auth requests weekly, each taking 20-30 minutes  
**Impact:**
- 3-5 hours weekly on insurance paperwork
- Treatment delays for patients (sometimes weeks)
- Patient frustration and complaints
- Disrupts clinic flow

**Current Workaround:** Delegate to MAs when possible, but still requires MD review and signature

### 3. Patient Portal Message Overload
**Problem:** 25-35 messages daily, often complex questions  
**Impact:**
- 1-2 hours daily responding to messages
- Interrupts evening and weekend time
- Some questions need in-person visit (revenue loss)
- Billing challenges (unpaid work)

**Current Workaround:** Batch responses after clinic, convert some to phone calls

### 4. ICD-10 Coding Complexity
**Problem:** Must select from 70,000+ diagnosis codes  
**Impact:**
- Claim denials due to incorrect/incomplete coding
- Time spent looking up correct codes
- Reduced reimbursement
- Documentation stress

**Current Workaround:** Reference guide and coding sheets, sometimes guess

### 5. Fragmented Patient Information
**Problem:** Data scattered across EMR, labs, imaging, specialists  
**Impact:**
- 5-10 minutes per patient gathering information
- Risk of missing critical information
- Duplicate testing ordered
- Patient safety concerns

**Current Workaround:** Manual chart review before each appointment

### 6. Prescription Management Inefficiency
**Problem:** Refills, prior auths, pharmacy calls interrupt workflow  
**Impact:**
- 15-20 interruptions per clinic session
- Lose train of thought during documentation
- Extends work day
- Medication errors when rushed

**Current Workaround:** Batch refills between patients, but urgent ones still interrupt

## 💬 Typical Day Workflow

**7:00 AM** - Arrive, review day's schedule, check overnight results  
**7:30 AM** - Pre-chart first few patients  
**8:00 AM** - Start patient appointments  
**8:15 AM** - See patient (15-min slot, often runs 20-25 minutes)  
**8:30 AM** - Document visit while prepping next patient chart  
**12:00 PM** - Working lunch (portal messages, refills, lab review)  
**1:00 PM** - Afternoon appointments begin  
**4:30 PM** - Last patient  
**5:00 PM** - Finish documentation, return calls  
**6:30 PM** - Still at office finishing charts and prior auths  
**7:00 PM** - Head home  
**8:00 PM** - More portal messages and chart finishing from home  

**Stress Points:** Running behind (every day after patient #3), lunch (no real break), after hours (unpaid work time)
