# Office Manager - Persona

## 👤 Profile

**Name:** Rebecca Thompson  
**Role:** Office Manager  
**Experience:** 15 years in healthcare administration  
**Department:** Administration  
**Reports To:** Practice Administrator / Owner  

## 📋 Responsibilities

### Primary Duties
- Oversee daily operations of the practice
- Staff scheduling and time-off coordination
- Budget management and expense tracking
- Vendor relations and contract negotiations
- Policy and procedure development
- Patient satisfaction monitoring
- Facility management and safety compliance
- HR functions (hiring, training, performance reviews)

### Secondary Duties
- Monthly financial reporting
- Quality improvement initiatives
- Staff meeting facilitation
- Regulatory compliance (OSHA, HIPAA)
- Emergency preparedness planning

## 😫 Daily Pain Points

### 1. Staff Scheduling Nightmare
**Problem:** Manual schedule creation for 120 employees with complex constraints  
**Impact:**
- 8-10 hours monthly creating schedules
- Frequent coverage gaps and conflicts
- Last-minute call-outs cause scrambling
- Overtime budget overruns (18% over budget)
- Staff complaints about fairness

**Current Workaround:** Excel spreadsheet, constant phone calls, manual adjustments

### 2. No Visibility into Operations
**Problem:** Data scattered across multiple systems with no dashboard  
**Impact:**
- Can't quickly answer leadership questions
- Reactive rather than proactive management
- Issues discovered too late
- Manual report compilation (full day monthly)
- Decision-making based on gut feel

**Current Workaround:** Weekly meetings to gather updates, manual data collection

### 3. Supply Chain Management
**Problem:** No automated inventory tracking or ordering  
**Impact:**
- Frequent emergency supply orders (higher costs)
- Staff interruptions about stockouts
- Over-ordering ties up cash
- Expired supplies written off monthly
- No vendor performance tracking

**Current Workaround:** Walk around checking supplies, sticky notes for orders

### 4. Patient Satisfaction Issues
**Problem:** Complaints come through multiple channels, no tracking  
**Impact:**
- Patient satisfaction score stuck at 3.8/5.0
- Don't know root causes of dissatisfaction
- Can't measure improvement efforts
- Risk of negative online reviews
- No systematic follow-up

**Current Workaround:** Respond to complaints as they come, no trend analysis

### 5. Staff Performance Management
**Problem:** No objective metrics for employee performance  
**Impact:**
- Difficult performance conversations
- Can't identify top performers vs. struggling staff
- Training needs unclear
- Retention issues (20% annual turnover)
- Compensation decisions subjective

**Current Workaround:** Annual reviews based on manager impressions

### 6. Financial Tracking Gaps
**Problem:** Month-end close takes 5 days, reports often inaccurate  
**Impact:**
- Leadership flying blind financially
- Can't quickly identify revenue/expense trends
- Billing issues discovered late
- Budget variance explanations difficult
- No real-time financial position

**Current Workaround:** Manual reconciliation, spreadsheet tracking

## 🎯 Agent Opportunities

**What it could do:**
- Auto-generate schedules based on:
  - Provider patient loads and specialties
  - Staff certifications and skills
  - Time-off requests and availability
  - Labor laws and break requirements
  - Historical volume patterns
- Handle call-outs and find coverage automatically
- Optimize for cost (minimize overtime)
- Fair distribution of desirable/undesirable shifts
- Send schedules to staff automatically

**Expected Impact:**
- Reduce scheduling time from 10 hours to 1 hour monthly
- Reduce overtime from 18% over budget to on-budget
- Eliminate coverage gaps
- Improve staff satisfaction with scheduling
- Better work-life balance for manager

- Automated monthly reporting

- Proactive problem identification
- Data-driven decision making
