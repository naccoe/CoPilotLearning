# Medical Assistant - Persona

## 👤 Profile

**Name:** Marcus Johnson  
**Role:** Lead Medical Assistant  
**Experience:** 5 years  
**Department:** Clinical Services  
**Reports To:** Nursing Manager  
**Certification:** Certified Medical Assistant (CMA)

## 📋 Responsibilities

### Primary Duties
- Room patients and collect vital signs
- Document chief complaints and medical history updates
- Prepare exam rooms and medical equipment
- Assist physicians during examinations
- Administer injections and vaccinations
- Perform basic lab procedures (fingersticks, rapid tests)
- Process prescription refill requests
- Manage clinical supply inventory

### Secondary Duties
- Schedule follow-up appointments
- Coordinate referrals to specialists
- Patient education on medications and care plans
- Call patients with lab results
- Clean and sterilize equipment

## 😫 Daily Pain Points

### 1. Prescription Refill Overload
**Problem:** 150+ daily refill requests via phone, fax, pharmacy calls  
**Impact:**
- 3-4 hours daily processing refills
- Takes time away from patient care
- Delays frustrate patients and pharmacies
- Risk of errors when rushed

**Current Workaround:** Batch process during lunch and after hours (overtime)

### 2. Vital Signs Documentation
**Problem:** Manual entry into EMR for every patient  
**Impact:**
- Double documentation (paper then computer)
- Transcription errors
- 5+ minutes per patient for data entry

**Current Workaround:** Write on paper during rooming, type in later

### 3. Lab Result Communication
**Problem:** Manually calling 20-30 patients daily with normal lab results  
**Impact:**
- Phone tag with patients
- Leaves voicemails with sensitive information
- Takes 1-2 hours daily
- Abnormal results sometimes delayed in pile

**Current Workaround:** Prioritize abnormal results, normals get delayed

### 4. Supply Inventory Chaos
**Problem:** No systematic tracking of clinical supplies  
**Impact:**
- Mid-day stockouts of critical items (needles, alcohol swabs)
- Over-ordering of slow-moving items
- Expired medications discovered during use
- Examination disruptions

**Current Workaround:** Ad-hoc inventory checks, emergency supply runs

### 5. Referral Coordination
**Problem:** Manual faxing of referrals and tracking appointments  
**Impact:**
- Patients wait weeks for specialist appointments
- Referrals lost or not followed up
- No visibility into which patients completed referrals
- Provider frustration

**Current Workaround:** Paper tracking log, often incomplete

### 6. Patient Flow Bottlenecks
**Problem:** No visibility into which patients are ready vs. waiting  
**Impact:**
- Providers waiting for rooms to be ready
- Patients waiting unnecessarily
- Inefficient room utilization
- Overtime due to appointment backups

**Current Workaround:** Walk around checking rooms constantly

**7:30 AM** - Review day's schedule, prepare exam rooms  
**8:00 AM** - Begin rooming patients (average 20-25 per day)  
**9:00 AM** - Pause to process prescription refills (30 min)  
**10:00 AM** - Continue rooming, administer vaccines  
**12:00 PM** - Quick lunch, more refill processing  
**1:00 PM** - Afternoon patient rooming  
**2:00 PM** - Call patients with lab results  
**3:00 PM** - Process referrals, coordinate follow-ups  
**4:00 PM** - Room late afternoon patients  
**5:00 PM** - Clean rooms, restock supplies, finish documentation  
**5:30 PM** - Often stay late to finish refills and callbacks  

**Stress Points:** Mid-morning (refill backlog), mid-afternoon (result calls), end of day (documentation catch-up)
